AUTONOMIC BEHAVIOR SYSTEM (ABS)
├── Entities
│   ├── Individual
│   ├── Habitual Responses
│   ├── Reflexes
│   ├── Emotional Triggers
│   ├── Environmental Stimuli
│   └── Basal Ganglia / Limbic System
├── Inputs
│   ├── Repeated Situational Triggers
│   ├── Emotional States (fear, comfort, stress)
│   ├── Physical Conditions (fatigue, hunger)
│   ├── Learned Associations (classical conditioning)
│   └── Environmental Cues (places, people, objects)
├── Outputs
│   ├── Habitual Actions (nail-biting, phone-checking)
│   ├── Reflexive Movements (blinking, pulling away from pain)
│   ├── Automatic Emotional Responses (anxiety in certain situations)
│   ├── Conditioned Behaviors (based on past experiences)
│   └── Stress Response Patterns (fight-or-flight reactions)
├── Dependencies
│   ├── Basal Ganglia (habit formation)
│   ├── Amygdala (emotional triggers)
│   ├── Limbic System (emotional memory)
│   └── Energy Conservation Needs
├── Boundaries
│   ├── Conscious vs. Unconscious Control
│   ├── Learned vs. Innate Behavior
│   ├── Adaptive vs. Maladaptive Habits
│   └── Internal vs. External Triggers
├── Modifiers
│   ├── Frequency of Exposure
│   ├── Strength of Emotional Association
│   ├── Stress Levels
│   ├── Changes in Environment
│   └── Age and Cognitive Maturity
├── Repositories
│   ├── Habit Loops (cue → routine → reward)
│   ├── Emotional Memories
│   ├── Motor Skill Memory
│   ├── Neural Pathways Reinforced by Repetition
│   └── Trauma-Related Conditioning
└── Processes
    ├── Trigger Detection (recognizing a familiar situation)
    ├── Routine Activation (automatically performing the behavior)
    ├── Reward Reception (feeling relief or pleasure)
    ├── Consolidation (strengthening the habit loop)
    ├── Repetition (making the behavior more automatic)
    ├── Disruption (breaking the cycle through awareness)
    └── Replacement (substituting new habits for old ones)
