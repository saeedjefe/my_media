VOLITIONAL PROCESS SYSTEM (VPS)
├── Entities
│   ├── Individual
│   ├── Goals
│   ├── Willpower
│   ├── Executive Function
│   ├── Motivation
│   └── Decision-Making Mechanism
├── Inputs
│   ├── Awareness of Goal
│   ├── Available Resources (time, energy, tools)
│   ├── Environmental Cues (triggers for action)
│   ├── Internal Desire (personal drive)
│   └── Feedback from Previous Actions
├── Outputs
│   ├── Conscious Decisions
│   ├── Goal-Directed Actions
│   ├── Delayed Gratification
│   ├── Self-Control
│   └── Adaptive Learning
├── Dependencies
│   ├── Working Memory
│   ├── Attention Span
│   ├── Emotional Regulation
│   └── Prior Knowledge or Experience
├── Boundaries
│   ├── Short-term vs. Long-term Goals
│   ├── Voluntary vs. Habitual Actions
│   ├── Personal Autonomy vs. External Influence
│   └── Ethical/Moral Constraints
├── Modifiers
│   ├── Fatigue or Stress
│   ├── External Distractions
│   ├── Reward/Punishment Structure
│   ├── Confidence and Self-Efficacy
│   └── Access to Role Models
├── Repositories
│   ├── Past Successes/Failures
│   ├── Strategies for Self-Control
│   ├── Value Systems and Beliefs
│   ├── Learned Techniques (e.g., time management)
│   └── Moral Frameworks
└── Processes
    ├── Goal Setting (defining desired outcome)
    ├── Planning (mapping steps and resources)
    ├── Initiation (starting despite resistance)
    ├── Maintenance (staying focused over time)
    ├── Self-Assessment (checking progress and motivation)
    ├── Adjustment (changing tactics based on feedback)
    ├── Completion or Abandonment (ending the process)
    └── Reflection (learning from experience)
