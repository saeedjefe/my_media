│   ├── Adaptation – revising instruction based on learner outcomes
│   ├── Maintenance (Homeostasis) – institutional stability and curriculum control

│   ├── Emergence – breakthrough learning, innovations in pedagogy

│   ├── Dissolution – dropout, school failure, curriculum decay
│   └── Distribution – delivery of education across demographics and locations
├── Behaviors (Systemic)
│   ├── Adaptation – Adjusting pedagogy or study methods based on performance
│   ├── Resilience – Persisting through academic difficulty or failure
│   ├── Vulnerability – Risk of dropout due to stress, poverty, or mismatch
│   ├── Rigidity – Resistance to pedagogical or cognitive innovation
│   ├── Cohesion – Alignment between learners, educators, and institutional goals
│   ├── Fragmentation – Curriculum disconnection or learning inequality
│   ├── Homogenization – Standardization of teaching at the expense of diversity
│   ├── Heterogenization – Personalized learning paths or differentiated instruction
│   ├── Assimilation – Integration of diverse students into shared learning culture
│   ├── Pluralism – Coexistence of various educational philosophies and approaches
│   ├── Dynamics – Evolution of methods, standards, and learning formats
│   ├── Statics – Institutional inertia or unchanging practices
│   ├── Emergence – Spontaneous discovery or breakthrough in learning
│   ├── Suppression – Overregulation or high-stakes testing stifling innovation
│   ├── Feedback Loops – Formative assessment, progress reports, revision cycles
│   ├── Repository Growth – Expansion of curriculum, tools, or intellectual resources
│   ├── Entropy – Confusion or decay due to lack of structure or poor policy
│   ├── Self-Improvement – Lifelong learning, upskilling, reflective practice
│   └── Delayed Gratification – Pursuit of long-term educational goals despite short-term cost
