POLLINATION SYSTEM (PS)
├── Entities
│   ├── Flowering Plants
│   ├── Pollinators
│   ├── Pollen
│   ├── Nectar & Scents
│   ├── Reproductive Structures
│   ├── Environmental Conditions
│   └── Human Influence
├── Inputs
│   ├── Pollen production
│   ├── Pollinator movement
│   ├── Nectar availability
│   ├── Flower color and scent
│   ├── Weather patterns
│   ├── Habitat quality
│   └── Agricultural influence
├── Outputs
│   ├── Fertilization
│   ├── Seed production
│   ├── Fruit development
│   ├── Genetic diversity
│   ├── Ecosystem support
│   ├── Human food supply
│   └── Pollinator decline (if disrupted)
├── Dependencies
│   ├── Presence of pollinators
│   ├── Biodiversity
│   ├── Floral resources
│   ├── Climate stability
│   └── Reduced pesticide use
├── Boundaries
│   ├── Geographical
│   ├── Temporal (seasonal)
│   ├── Ecological (species-specific interaction)
│   ├── Species boundaries (some pollinators only work with certain plants)
│   └── Human impact (farming, urbanization)
├── Modifiers
│   ├── Climate change
│   ├── Urban expansion
│   ├── Monoculture farming
│   ├── Pesticide use
│   ├── Conservation efforts
│   └── Artificial pollination technology
├── Repositories
│   ├── Floral memory (pollinators)
│   ├── Gene banks
│   ├── Ecological records
│   ├── Scientific research
│   └── Traditional agricultural knowledge
└── Processes
    ├── Input acquisition (pollen, nectar, pollinators)
    ├── Attraction (color, scent)
    ├── Transfer (pollen movement)
    ├── Fertilization
    ├── Feedback loop (reward → future visits)
    ├── Maintenance (repeating each season)
    ├── Replication (across plants and pollinators)
    └── Dissolution (loss due to environmental damage)
