HABITAT DESTRUCTION SYSTEM (HDS)
├── Entities
│   ├── Forests, wetlands, coral reefs
│   ├── Human activity (logging, construction)
│   ├── Wildlife species
│   ├── Government planners
│   ├── Local communities
│   └── Conservation groups
├── Inputs
│   ├── Deforestation
│   ├── Urban expansion
│   ├── Agriculture conversion
│   ├── Mining or drilling operations
│   ├── Infrastructure projects
│   └── Climate-related events
├── Outputs
│   ├── Species displacement
│   ├── Biodiversity loss
│   ├── Fragmented habitats
│   ├── Ecosystem collapse
│   ├── Human-wildlife conflict
│   └── Policy responses
├── Dependencies
│   ├── Economic development goals
│   ├── Land-use planning
│   ├── Population growth
│   ├── Lack of protected areas
│   └── Weak enforcement of laws
├── Boundaries
│   ├── Protected vs. unprotected land
│   ├── Natural vs. human-made boundaries
│   ├── Coastal vs. inland habitat
│   ├── Legal protections
│   └── Ecological thresholds
├── Modifiers
│   ├── Technological advancement
│   ├── Corporate influence
│   ├── Public awareness
│   ├── Policy changes
│   ├── Migration of humans or animals
│   └── Climate change effects
├── Repositories
│   ├── Satellite imagery
│   ├── Environmental assessments
│   ├── Historical ecosystem maps
│   ├── NGO reports
│   └── Government archives
└── Processes
    ├── Input acquisition (land clearing)
    ├── Transformation (habitat fragmentation)
    ├── Output production (loss of species)
    ├── Feedback loop (ecosystem imbalance)
    ├── Maintenance (partial recovery)
    ├── Replication (new destruction sites)
    ├── Dissolution (complete loss of habitat)
    └── Restoration attempts
