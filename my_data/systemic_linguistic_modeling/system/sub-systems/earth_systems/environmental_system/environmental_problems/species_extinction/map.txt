SPECIES EXTINCTION SYSTEM (SES)
├── Entities
│   ├── Endangered species
│   ├── Ecosystem
│   ├── Human activities (hunting, deforestation)
│   ├── Climate change
│   ├── Invasive species
│   ├── Government agencies
│   └── Conservation organizations
├── Inputs
│   ├── Habitat destruction
│   ├── Overhunting/poaching
│   ├── Climate shifts
│   ├── Introduction of invasive species
│   ├── Pollution
│   ├── Illegal wildlife trade
│   └── Agricultural expansion
├── Outputs
│   ├── Complete extinction
│   ├── Genetic diversity loss
│   ├── Food chain disruption
│   ├── Loss of ecological services
│   ├── Collapse of ecosystems
│   └── Ethical/public concern
├── Dependencies
│   ├── Habitat availability
│   ├── Conservation efforts
│   ├── Policy enforcement
│   ├── Public support
│   └── Funding for research
├── Boundaries
│   ├── Natural range of species
│   ├── Political borders
│   ├── Time-based (fast vs. slow extinction)
│   ├── Geographic isolation
│   └── Species type (plants vs. animals)
├── Modifiers
│   ├── Climate change
│   ├── Technological intervention
│   ├── Laws and treaties
│   ├── Public campaigns
│   ├── Trade restrictions
│   └── Restoration programs
├── Repositories
│   ├── IUCN Red List
│   ├── Zoological records
│   ├── Historical species accounts
│   ├── Conservation databases
│   └── Legal agreements (CITES, CBD)
└── Processes
    ├── Input acquisition (pressures on species)
    ├── Transformation (population decline)
    ├── Output production (extinction)
    ├── Feedback loop (awareness and response)
    ├── Maintenance (conservation actions)
    ├── Replication (other species affected)
    ├── Dissolution (species lost forever)
    └── Recovery attempts (captive breeding, reintroduction)
