WATER CONTAMINATION SYSTEM (WCS)
├── Entities
│   ├── Pollutants (plastics, chemicals, pathogens)
│   ├── Industrial facilities
│   ├── Agricultural runoff
│   ├── Urban waste systems
│   ├── Aquatic ecosystems
│   ├── Government regulators
│   └── Consumers
├── Inputs
│   ├── Industrial discharge
│   ├── Fertilizer/pesticide runoff
│   ├── Sewage overflow
│   ├── Plastic waste
│   ├── Mining or oil spill contaminants
│   └── Household chemicals
├── Outputs
│   ├── Drinking water contamination
│   ├── Fish death or mutation
│   ├── Algal blooms
│   ├── Loss of biodiversity
│   ├── Human illness
│   └── Regulatory action
├── Dependencies
│   ├── Waste management infrastructure
│   ├── Water treatment systems
│   ├── Rainfall/runoff patterns
│   ├── Industrial practices
│   └── Public hygiene habits
├── Boundaries
│   ├── Groundwater vs. surface water
│   ├── Rural vs. urban water systems
│   ├── Legal jurisdiction (local vs. national)
│   ├── Short-term vs. long-term contamination
│   └── Domestic vs. industrial use
├── Modifiers
│   ├── Climate change (floods/droughts)
│   ├── Population density
│   ├── Technological progress
│   ├── Policy changes
│   ├── Global trade
│   └── Education level of public
├── Repositories
│   ├── Water quality reports
│   ├── Government databases
│   ├── Scientific studies
│   ├── Historical usage patterns
│   └── Public awareness campaigns
└── Processes
    ├── Input entry (contaminant release)
    ├── Transport through water bodies
    ├── Bioaccumulation in food chain
    ├── Detection and testing
    ├── Public reaction
    ├── Legislative response
    ├── Remediation
    ├── Replication (in new areas)
    └── Dissolution (clean-up success/failure)
